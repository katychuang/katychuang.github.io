Food experiement

Posted on November 9, 2010 with labels: editorial

Woke up today craving homecooked taiwanese food of my childhood, which I cannot find in Philly (heard of a secret Taiwanese menu at Empress Garden, but didn’t want to make a trip today). In case you are unaware, Taiwanese culture has a mentality that the best things in life are free. That phrase simplifies of a long history of a financially poor country that bred people to value industriousness and frugality. Having said that, I am so Taiwanese because I mentally calculated the time/financial costs before deciding to cook steamed pork with salted eggs. With my usual proclivity towards efficiency, my decision was based primarily upon the advantages in terms of nutrition and amount of food per dollar as well as overhead such as amount of dishes needed and to wash afterwards. Taste is also an important criteria of course, but as a grad student that comes second to time/money.

Thinking like a scientist for this experiment! Came up with a specific objective, approach for meeting the objective, and rationale. Even thought it’s been months since I’ve cooked anything (don’t have kitchen anymore), it didn’t stop me. I resorted to rice cookers.

  • Objective: Create a nutritious, flavorful meal that is also budget friendly.
  • Motivation: Desire for food that will give me energy through studying and cold weather, but isn’t fattening.

  • Reviewed ‘literature’ for comparison on ingredients and methods:

    • Hakka style steamed salted egg with pork
    • Ground pork and salted egg

  • Approach:

    • Ingredients: Mix pork, Salt, Garlic, White Pepper, Sesame Oil. Layer 4 eggs on top.
    • Apparatus: 2 rice cookers (1 for rice and 1 for pork&eggs)

  • Cook time: 20 mins unattended

  • Results:
    • Meals: Enough food for at least 3 meals. (to be mixed with various veggies/soups)
    • Cost Breakdown Pork – $2.49 (at $2.29/lb) Eggs – $0.33 (at $0.99/dz) Rice, Garlic, White pepper, sesame oil (under $3)

  • Conclusion: for approximately $5 and an hour of my time and under $10 in my pocket, I can create 3 delicious meals!!
