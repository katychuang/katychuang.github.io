Fashion Perspective

Posted on September 11, 2010 with labels: fashion

Shoes

Overhearing some girls chat about shoe sales made me see that my interests in ‘fashion’ is a different concept…. They were concerned about acquiring products for their collections. I tend to analyze items for their functionality, durability, and usability. This is probably due to my background in software design and information design.

-> two tangent thoughts. back in MLIS program I liked the course projects that involved creativity, such as creating programs, finding solutions to problems, and teaching people how to find information. I did not enjoy cataloging as much. Wearing the ‘information detective’ hat was more fun because you don’t know what to expect.

All the books I read the past spring about fashion merchandising were useful in understanding the fashion industry. however, much of it was about making profits and about moving products. It doesn’t sit right with me that the business side of the industry focuses so highly on ephemeral items. sometimes materialistic culture feels wrong. i do like the artistic, anything-goes aspect of the industry. my conclusion after a long bout of reflection: i’m interested in clothing as costumes, as a message. impressions create mental models (i.e. stereotypes). heh, back to communication design.
