Female PhDs = Aliens

Posted on September 11, 2010 with labels: beauty

In Chinese culture, any women that get PhDs are considered to be aliens, **“such women were perceived as not being able to get men.**” Getting past ‘Female PhD’ Label

Loveable aliens

It’s a horrible stereotype. Many women are worried that they won’t find a husband to marry in their 20s (I assume it’s because they don’t realize they have nearly the same earning potential). I deal with my family/friends commenting that I will never find a man that would be willing to marry me AND deal with men who feel emasculated because I’ll point out flaws in their logic. How could I be intimidating? I’m a petite, asian girl.

Of all the women on US Supreme court, they’re either unmarried or divorced? “In its 220-year history, only three women justices have ever served on the Supreme Court: Sandra Day O’Connor (1981-2005); Ruth Bader Ginsburg (1993-present) and most recently Sonia Sotomayor.” ( History of Women on the Supreme Court) Most of the men were married. It goes to show how unbalanced the gender divide is absolutely unfair. Another example: Oprah. For her to do all the good she does to everyone, she chose career over kids and marriage. Why do women have to make a choice when men usually don’t have same pressure?

Supreme Court Justices

One of my lifetime goals (out of many other random ideas) is to demonstrate to the world that there is no direct correlation between attractiveness and education level. Women don’t automatically become frumpy when they go for PhDs (though it’s possible that personal grooming becomes less of a priority). Good looking women can be smart too. There is no shame in having brains AND looking attractive AND contributing to society. IMO, a woman with these three qualities has the best shot of achieving world peace.
